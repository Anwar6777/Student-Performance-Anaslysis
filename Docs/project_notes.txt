Project Insights / Findings

Student Performance Analysis — Key Insights
1. Overall academic performance is moderate
The average overall student score is 64.02
Pass percentage is 88.03%, indicating that most students meet the minimum passing criteria, though high distinction performance is still limited
2. Math is the strongest-performing subject, while English is the weakest
Math has the highest average subject score
English has the lowest average subject score
The gap is not extremely large, but it suggests English may require more academic support or intervention compared to Math and Science
3. Study hours show a positive relationship with overall score
Students with higher study hours generally tend to achieve higher overall scores
This suggests consistent study time is associated with better academic outcomes
4. Attendance is positively associated with performance
Students with higher attendance generally perform better overall
Low attendance appears more common among lower-performing students, indicating attendance may be a meaningful performance driver
5. Parent education appears to have some influence on student performance
Students whose parents have higher educational backgrounds (such as diploma/postgraduate) show slightly higher average scores compared to students from less formally educated backgrounds
However, the performance gap is moderate rather than extreme
6. Internet access has a small but positive effect
Students with internet access perform slightly better on average than students without internet access
This may indicate the benefit of digital learning resources, though the gap is relatively small
7. Extra activities do not significantly reduce academic performance
Students participating in extra activities perform at a very similar level to those who do not
This suggests extracurricular involvement may not harm academic performance and may coexist with balanced academic outcomes
8. Study method differences are visible but not dramatic
Students using online videos and coaching show slightly higher average scores
Group study appears comparatively lower in this dataset
The differences are not large enough to claim causation, but they do suggest study approach may influence outcomes
9. Top-performing students show a combination of high attendance and high study hours
Many top students have:
very high attendance
study hours near the upper range
grade A
This reinforces the relationship between discipline, attendance, and strong academic performance
10. Lowest-performing students generally show low study hours and lower attendance
The lowest-performing student records tend to have:
low study hours
lower attendance percentages
failing grades
This highlights a potential target group for academic intervention
